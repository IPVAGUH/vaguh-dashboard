Direction for the Videographer

You are responsible for B-roll, behind-the-scenes footage, and editing the long form video (Full Build Ep. 1). Jeremy and Adam will direct you throughout the day. Here is what you need to know.

This is our first time working together. Ask questions if anything is unclear. Jeremy and Adam will tell you what to shoot and when. Your main job is capturing great B-roll, behind-the-scenes moments, and cinematic shots. The install instruction footage will mostly come from Jeremy and Adam's phone cameras.

Your Role

B-roll: Wide shots of the shop (Achtuning), the car, the parts, tool close-ups, hands working, anything visually interesting.

Behind-the-scenes: Candid moments, banter between Jeremy and Adam, reactions, struggles, wins. The stuff that makes the video feel real.

Cinematic shots: Beauty shots of the car, slow pans across parts, the Achtuning space. These are the "breathing room" moments in the edit.

Editing: You will edit the long form build episode using all footage (your camera + Jeremy and Adam's phone footage).

Long Form Video Structure

The video should feel like a Mat Armstrong or B is for Build episode. The tone shifts by section:

Intro/unboxing: Casual, personality-driven. Jokes, mistakes, banter. Quick cuts, energy, music. Show off Achtuning.

Sway bar & control arms: More instructional but still fun. Viewer should follow along, but this does NOT need to be as detailed as the standalone DIY videos. Condense into highlights with personality.

Tune & intake: Casual vlog energy. Focus on reactions and sounds.

Test drive & wrap: High energy, reactions, good music. This is the payoff.

Parallel Installs -- How to Handle

Jeremy and Adam will each be doing an install at the same time. Float between them and grab B-roll, reactions, and behind-the-scenes moments. You do NOT need continuous coverage of both installs. Jeremy and Adam are handling the step-by-step instruction footage on their phones. Focus on the stuff they can't capture themselves: wide angles, both of them working at once, candid moments.

Jeremy and Adam handle the standalone DIY video edits separately using their phone footage. You are focused on the long form build episode only. Don't stress about capturing every install step. Focus on B-roll, energy, and the moments that make the video entertaining.
