Edit Notes for Brendan

Overall Editing Style (Mat Armstrong reference)

  • Narration approach: Hybrid -- use on-camera talking wherever it exists. Fill gaps with voiceover from Jeremy/Adam. Never go more than 20-30 seconds without someone explaining what's happening.
    Mat Armstrong uses post-edit voiceover constantly to bridge between on-camera moments. Flag any gaps longer than 30 seconds for VO recording.
  • Pacing rule: If nothing interesting is happening on screen for more than 5 seconds, cut. Use time-lapses, speed ramps, or jump cuts to compress repetitive work.
  • Audio layering: Keep real garage sounds (ratchets, impacts, grinder) audible underneath the music. Don't bury them.
    Mat Armstrong's videos always have workshop sounds mixed in. It makes the viewer feel like they're there.
  • Reactions are gold: Every genuine laugh, frustrated sigh, or "oh no" moment is usable. Cut toward personality.
  • B-roll as breathing room: Between talking segments, drop in 3-5 second B-roll shots (tools, car details, 034 branding, Achtuning shop).
